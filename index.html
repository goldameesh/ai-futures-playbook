About This Playbook

Foreword by Amish Shah

In a world where artificial intelligence is reshaping every facet of life‚Äîfrom how we learn to how we work‚Äîpreparing our children isn't just an option; it's essential.

As an educational futurist, I've dedicated my career to bridging technology and human potential. This playbook draws from global best practices, including the World Economic Forum's insights on the future of jobs and pioneering curricula in forward-thinking nations like the UAE and India.

üõ°Ô∏è Child Safety Guidelines for AI Tools

Essential rules when children use AI tools:

  • ‚úì Under-13s: Use only under parent supervision with parent-managed accounts
  • ‚úì Respect age limits: Always follow each provider's minimum age requirements
  • ‚úì No personal information: Never upload photos, addresses, phone numbers, or school names
  • ‚úì Disable data training: Opt out of data collection where possible
  • ‚úì Review activity regularly: Check what your child is creating and sharing
  • ‚úì Use school/family accounts: Avoid personal accounts for young children
  • ‚úì Teach digital citizenship: Discuss privacy, consent, and responsible use

COPPA Compliance (USA)

For children under 13 in the USA, ensure any online service is COPPA-compliant and uses verifiable parental consent.

GDPR/GDPR-K (EU/UK)

For children in EU/UK, services must comply with GDPR and obtain parental consent for children under 16 (or lower age in some countries).

UAE Regulations

Follow UAE's Child Rights Law and ensure services comply with local data protection requirements.

India IT Rules

Comply with India's IT Rules 2021 regarding children's data and parental consent requirements.

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

How do I verify if a course is legitimate and properly licensed?

For UAE courses: Check KHDA's official website (khda.gov.ae) for registered training centers. Look for KHDA approval certificates on the provider's website.

For India courses: While NCERT doesn't certify private courses, look for ISO certification, reviews from verified parents, and check if the provider is registered as an educational institution.

For all courses: Read recent reviews (within 6 months), verify instructor credentials, check refund policies, and start with a free trial if available.

Are the "free" courses really free, or are there hidden costs?

Courses labeled "FREE" in this playbook are free to access as of December 2025, but always verify current terms:

  • Code.org, MIT Day of AI, Hour of AI, AI4K12, Elements of AI: Completely free with no hidden costs
  • Google AI Essentials: Typically requires Coursera subscription (~$49/month), but may be free via some schools/employers
  • Some providers: May offer free basic tiers with paid upgrades for certificates or advanced features

Always read the fine print before enrolling.

How much should I expect to spend on my child's AI education?

You can complete the entire 3-year journey for FREE using Code.org, MIT Day of AI, AI4K12, and Elements of AI.

If you choose paid options:

  • Budget: $40-80/month (JetLearn, Codingal basic tier)
  • Mid-range: $200-400 for structured programs (Henry Harvin, UAE centers)
  • Premium: $900-1,850+ for intensive programs (Inspirit AI)

Start free, then invest in paid courses only if your child shows sustained interest.

What if pricing or availability changes after I read this playbook?

Course pricing, availability, and features change frequently. This playbook is accurate as of December 2025, but:

  • Always verify current pricing directly with the provider
  • Check for seasonal discounts or promotions
  • Ask about scholarships or financial aid
  • Subscribe to our quarterly updates for the latest information

We update this playbook every 3 months, but cannot guarantee real-time accuracy.
